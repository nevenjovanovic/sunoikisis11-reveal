Croatia

Separated from Yugoslavia in 1991, a member of the EU from 2013. Population 4,284,889 (2011). About 27,000 of them study Latin in school. GDP $13,994 p. c. (2015 est.)

Croatian Latin

croala.ffzg.unizg.hr/intro

bitbucket.org/nevenjovanovic/croalatxt

Over five million words (and growing) of medieval and early modern Latin texts connected with Croatia, from the 10th to 20th century, encoded as TEI XML, licensed CC-BY.

Currently in transition.

Served on the internet by PhiloLogic 3 and PhiloLogic 4 (solr.ffzg.hr/philo4/croala0/). Also as a BaseX XML database (solr.ffzg.hr/basex/).

CroALa is a research tool requiring knowledge of Latin, forming hypothesis of what Croatian Latin is, and finding courage to ask: what do you do with five million unknown Latin words?

Inscriptions

In Croatia, they are all around us — easy to engage with.

CIL IV 8707: Spalati calle Cambi in angulo domus n. 141.

A guerilla approach: find an inscription, transcribe it, translate it, photograph it, note its GPS coordinates, publish it on the internet like this. Eventually, add a note of dignity to being a tourist in Croatia.

Dubrovnik, 21 June 2014.

Doing things with Greek and Latin

Croatia is reforming its educational curriculum. What will happen to Greek and Latin? Will the languages be jettisoned for utilitarian reasons?

We want to be prepared for this discussion — by having means to engage people, projects to engage them on, and international partnerships to make it all more stimulating.

Sunoikisis is best done together!
